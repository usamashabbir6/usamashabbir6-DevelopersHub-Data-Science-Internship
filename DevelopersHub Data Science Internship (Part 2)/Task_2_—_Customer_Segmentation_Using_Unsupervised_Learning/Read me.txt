✅ Task 2 — Customer Segmentation Using Unsupervised Learning


Approach:


Performed EDA on Age, Annual Income, and Spending Score
Used the Elbow Method to determine the optimal number of clusters
Applied K-Means clustering on Income and Spending Score
Used PCA to visualize clusters when adding Age as a third dimension
Profiled each cluster and proposed a tailored marketing strategy


Key Insights:


5 distinct customer segments identified, ranging from premium
high-income/high-spending customers to disengaged low-income/low-spending customers
Largest segment: Average Income, Average Spending (Standard Customers)
Smallest but highest-value segment: High Income, High Spending (Premium Customers)
Gender showed minimal correlation with spending behavior — income and
spending score were the true drivers of segmentation


Marketing Strategies Proposed:


Premium customers → VIP loyalty programs, early access to new products
Cautious savers (high income, low spending) → personalized conversion offers
Budget-conscious spenders (low income, high spending) → installment plans, discount bundles
Standard customers → seasonal promotions, email newsletters
Low engagement customers → low-cost retention campaigns


Skills Demonstrated:


Unsupervised learning (K-Means)
Dimensionality reduction (PCA)
Customer segmentation
Strategy development based on data insights
